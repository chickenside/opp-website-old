About The Prowl

The Skinny

The Ogden Park Prowlis a neighborhood art festival in Mid City Baton Rouge. Come Prowlabout our neighborhood September, 27 from 1-6pm.

History of the Prowl

Back in the olden days, before the invention of art, people from all over would flock to our humble lil holler’ of Ogden Park just to enjoy the fine company and high spirits of the gentle folk who live here. They came for the feasting and the toasting and the long evenings on back porches. And you just know they kept coming back.

Well, after perfecting the art of “a good time,” we reckoned that we might better stay ahead of the game and perfect some other groovy thing before we end up like the Roman Empire. The Prowlis that other groovy thing!

See, last year we partnered up with Elevator Projects. They’re a top-notch group of creative artistic types who helped us organize artists who live in our neighborhood with other artists in the area. And knowing as how Ogden Park is squarely situated in the Mid City Arts district, and also knowing that all sales of art in said district are tax-free, we thought our neighborhood would present an awful fine place for an Arts Festival.

So this is what we did. We loaded up our neighborhood with artists, craftspeople and showoffs from around the area. We set up shop in our homes and on our yards. And the people came to prowl. We put out signs so they’d know where to go. Folks bought art and crafts. They gandered at prize gardens. They sampled culinary mastery and you just know we had a band.

We had strangers in the holler’ and they prowled up and down our streets, into our houses and onto our dang lawns and it was insanely groovy!

Now, since nobody got dog-bit or struck by a comet, and since most everybody who prowled got home before dinner, we figured we just might be on to something. So we’re doing it again.

Boom! Is your mind now blown? Does it now wonder what kind of arts and crafts we're talkig about here? Hmm, let's see now. We’ve got watercolor, oil and acrylic paintings. We’ll throw in some portraiture, pen-and-ink, charcoal and ceramics. Metalwork, furniture, jewelry and sculpture may be prowled as well. Plus, we have a bevy of conspicuously dandy businesses right in the holler’ who would be quite tickled to have you Prowlabout their concerns.

And you just know we’ve got a neighborhood bar. For those among you stricken with bilious colic or other afflictive vexations of the gullet, digestive spirits will be made available to you at a modest outlay. Please prowl responsibly!

We hope you and your’s will come Prowlaround with us this September, 27 from 1-6pm.
